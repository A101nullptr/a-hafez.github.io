    Tactile Symbiosis

    Skyscraper | Competition

    Challenging the boundaries of what a skyscraper is or should be, in terms of either its form or function. Seeking to rethink the notion of fixed vertical structures presented as monolithic monuments, existing independently from there context as is often the case, into a more fluid, tactile design approach that is in itself, still is a narrative of intervention on the prevailing context as its predecessor, yet doesn't super impose its existence on it nor seeks to disconnect from it. Establishing a Symbiotic relationship that is codependent on its surrounding context in order to truly exist.

    Apartment Redesign

    Interior | Competition

    Here the focus was on creating an open space in the common areas of the house, while still establishing an indirect separation between spaces by using very calm and subtle materials. The main color incorporated in the design is the natural wood color with the combination of whites for the walls and ceiling will using beige tiles for the flooring in some areas and in other using lightly stained wooden plankes.

    Off The Page

    Kiosk | Competition

    Moving away from the rigidity and sharpness of a cubic shape; a free flowing and versatile shape is designed to fit public space users and attract the targeted crowd. The reading portal's adaptable structure allows for its situation along different routes and in different spaces; the roof could be equipped with anti-water materials for rainy seasons, and open in summer season.

    Through The Box

    Installation | Competition

    Programming the urbanscapes of Amman. As Urbanists or "Flâneurs" we have been used to wandering the streets, noting, listening, and watching with no ulterior motive rather than to observe society and the city. You can see how the city developed from the small details of the sidewalks or the cracked shutters of an old Ammani house.
