    Reading Portal

    Year | 2019 | Competition
    Type | Kiosk

    Place & Concept | Three main public spaces were chosen to accommodate our reading portal. A park with its free flow movement, usually luxuriously paced users; the train stations with narrow platforms and rushed axial way of movement; and the Plazas with their chaotic feel and random paths.

    1. Accommodating the circulation patterns in our chosen public spaces the reading portal allows for free movement through and around it, as it acts as an entryway and a by-space as well.

    2. Recessions and extrusions are created to provide two seating spaces, and two easy to grab books cases.

    3. Structure is adapted in height and width to create a comfortable, yet meditative secure space. Areas where shading is needed are pin-pointed and the structure is adapted accordingly.

    4. Moving away from the rigidity and sharpness of a cubic shape; a free flowing and versatile shape is designed to fit public space users and attract the targeted crowd. Areas where structural support might be needed are highlighted in blue, where bicycle racks are added in which they have both functional and structural importance.

    5. Bike racks and book cases are placed in the structure; blue lines indicate the solid structure. This is later on divided to allow for a more playful play on the rigidity of the previous structure.

    6. The structure is now exposed, allowing it to be more transparent and versatile. Spaces between panels follow the book case divisions and are amended to accommodate a leaning wall for quick reads!

    7. The reading portal's adaptable structure allows for its situation along different routes and in different spaces; the roof could be equipped with anti-water materials for rainy seasons, and open in summer season.

    | Structural Fabrication |

    The sturcture is made primarily of recycled compressed wood boards with a handfull of recycled insulation and damping-proof materials. Connected by recycled steel rods fitted with washers and wooden spacers. 1. Waterproofing paint. 2. Cylindrical Wooden spacer. 3. Steel Washer. 4. 1.2 cm Nails. 5. Connecting 2 cm diameter Steel Rod. 6. Wooden Stud. 7. Recycled Compressed wood boards. 8. Recycled Plastic sheets - Damping Proof layer. 9. Recycled Foam boards.

    | Bookcase Assembly |

    Consisting of two bookcases, located opposite each other near the entrances of the structure. Formed from the structure's vertical panels that act as the dividers then sliding the horizontal shelves into the precut slits of the panels. Creating four cavities in the lower shelf area for housing small Boston ferns. Accessible from both inside and outside of the structure. With steel bicycle racks fixed to it on both sides located undeneath the bookcase area in order to support it’s weight.

    | Base materials & Bicycle racks |

    The structure of the reading room extends to its base, with recycled compressed wood, steel bars and recycled rubber matts that together make up the floor materials with linear 5-6.25 cm wide strips. The bike rack bars extend the width of the structure, with three bars at each side connected by a base bar. The racks could host a maximum of 8 bikes packed tightly, so 4 would be the comfortable amount.

    | Lighting Components |

    Containing 6 LED light strips each inside of a stanless steel linear housing. Powered the grid based on the place the structure is in. Providing low energy consumption for the project, while maintaining the design’s efficientcy and sustainability.

    | Seating & Leaning areas |

    Designed initially for outdoor use with the addition of an inner leaning area, consisting of rubber and polyester white fabric that makes up the cushions. Filling in the gaps between the wood panels and fixed in place by being wrapped inbetween the wooden spacers and the shelves of the structure.

    | Shading Mechanism |

    Made of wrapped, recycled, white strips of polyester fabric, providing water-proofing for the structure and sun protection for the books. The fabric is set above the roof, extending on both sides of it and ending with manually adjusted lengths based on rollers. Cut into multiple strips of mostly 6.25 cm in width as well as some others with varying dimensions. Also having slits in them so as to be placed inbetween lights.

    | Usability Analysis |

    The linear circulation in the portal is directed through two entrances. The interior space consists of two parabolic bookcases extending outwards and two area with foam padding for people to lean against, used while reading or searching for a book. Both mirrored horizontally and vertically from the structure’s center lines, which was done to establish comfort and ease of use, so no one area is far from a user. The desicion for having the bookcase extend outwards, was so that the portal would be accessible from the outside as well. This provided the base points for locating the bicycle racks that also act as the vertical structural support for the portal. The seating areas of the project are located outdoors in a shaded design that consists of an overhange for part of the structure, covered with protective layering.

    | Design Mobility |

    Transportation of the structure would be fairly easy by just strapping it to a truck either fully assembled or disassembled into pieces and constructing it on site.

    | Space Adaptability |

    The portal is suggested to be in an open public space. Thats partially, due to the large open space needed for the full structure to be hosted in, while maintaing user comfort. However because of the structure’s construction system, this isn’t an issue as it can be half the proposed length, depending on the space requirments.

    | Suggested Application |

    The entry elevations could be utillized to fuction as ad boards displaying ads in strips placed on the first three wooden panel sides.
